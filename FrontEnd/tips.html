Geography & Climate

Spain's geography is as diverse and varied as the country. While urban and rugged mountain chains dot the Pyrenees and Sierra Nevada.

Northern Spain: Cooler, greener (Atlantic climate)

Central Spain: Hot summers, cold winters (Continental climate)

Southern & Eastern coasts: Warm, Mediterranean climate

Canary Islands: Subtropical; eternal spring weather

Current Affairs (2025)

Politics: Spain is in awareness of its ongoing tensions with new far-left turn voters.

Justice: Generally safe, though pickpocketing remains common in crowded tourist zones.

Immigration & Travel: Stricter processes for tourists and locals, with stricter passport limits on entry and movement of newcomers.

Sustainability Focus: Major cities are implementing "green" travel measures (electric buses, bike rentals, car-free zones).

Customs ,Etiquette and Traditions

Spring (April - June)

Weather: Mild and pleasant. The country is in bloom.

Highlights: Major religious and cultural festivities like Seville's Feria de Abril and the Semana Santa (Holy Week) processions.

Local Customs: This is the perfect weather for your first terraza (terrace) drink. Spaniards love takers and a light jacket for the cooler evenings.

Summer (July - August)

Weather: Hot and lively, especially in the south and interior.

Highlights: Major city festivals like the Running of the Bulls (July 6-14) in Pamplona and La Tomatina, plus vibrant beach life and nightlife.

Local Customs: This is when Spanish schedules make perfect sense.

Siesta: In many towns (and even some city shops), expect businesses to close from 2 PM to 5 PM to escape the intense heat.

Social Life: Life happens after dark as families hit the streets in the air to cool. Pack light, breathable fabrics (like linen) to stay comfortable.

Autumn (September - October)

Weather: Warm and scenic, with comfortable temperatures.

Highlights: Wine harvest festivals like La Rioja's Fiesta de San Mateo or La Mercè in Barcelona, hiking, and enjoying fall harvests after the summer rush.

Local Customs: The "siesta" rhythm may persist in smaller towns, but the pace is more relaxed. It's an ideal time for long, casual dinners as the evenings are still beautiful. Pack layers, like a sweater or light coat.

Winter (November - February)

Weather: Cooler in the north (like the Pyrenees), mild on the southern coast (like Andalusia).

Highlights: Skiing, festive Christmas markets, elaborate nativity scenes, and the world-famous Carnival of Santa Cruz de Tenerife.

Local Customs: Pack a good coat, boots, and scarves to bundle up, festive dinners and Christmas markets come to life in the evenings.

And no mejor the season! A friendly "Hola" or "buenas" is always the right way to greet someone.

Safety, Health & Travel Tips

Generally safe-be street-smart in tourist-heavy (Barcelona, Madrid).

Use crossbody bags and keep passports secured.

Emergency: 112 (works EU-wide)

Healthcare: EU citizens can use the EHIC card; pharmacies are well-stocked.
